About Me

Hello! I'm Talha Siddique.

I work at the intersection of remote sensing, embedded systems, and machine learning, with a focus on building intelligent systems that operate reliably on low-power, resource-constrained hardware. Currently, I’m working as a postdoctoral research associate, where I’m involved in three distinct projects: autonomous path planning for unmanned surface vehicles (USVs); semantic and spatial reasoning over electronic navigation charts (ENCs) using large language models (LLMs); real-time bathymetric data processing pipelines. Previously, my Ph.D. research focused on building a machine learning-enabled magnetometer system for real-time space weather prediction. With a background that combines computer science and computational economics, I aim to bring a cross-disciplinary approach to solving complex problems.

Outside of work, I’m an avid fan of motorsports—particularly Formula 1—and also a follower of mixed martial arts.
